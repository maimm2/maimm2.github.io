I am a Computational Linguistics PhD candidate at University of Illinois Urbana Champaign, and I work with Dr. Lane Schwartz and Dr. Nizar Habash . My interests include computationally modeling low-resource languages, and my dissertation focuses on classifying and modeling the morphosyntax and morphophonology of Egyptian Arabic dialectal varieties. My research approach centers on bridging the divide between computational and linguistic literature in computational modeling of Egyptian Arabic. It centers around leveraging linguistic literature to inform and facilitate computational modeling of Egyptian Arabic, with a strong emphasis on diversifying data sources for better representation and creating tools and resources that directly benefit the Egyptian community.

I am a firm believer that the focus on high-resource languages in NLP inadvertently sustains a form of gatekeeping that limits the availability of knowledge, information, accessibility tools, and educational resources for monolingual speakers of low-resource languages, while also reinforcing an ideology of superiority of certain languages over others. As a computational linguist, I am driven by my responsibility to care about the languages and the communities within which these languages are spoken above all else when working on low-resource languages.

I earned my MA in teaching English as a Second Language from UIUC in 2020. I also graduated with a Bachelors in English Literature, Linguistics, and Education from Suez Canal University, Ismailia, Egypt in 2016 (Ranked 1st in class). My research experience includes experimental research exploring mental representation of time among Arabic and English bilinguals advised by Dr. Ionin. I have also researched second language acquisition among Egyptian Twitter users advised by Dr. Sadler and Dr. Yan.

I co-chaired the Annual Illinois Language and Linguistics Society Conference, which took place at UIUC on March 3rd, and March 4th, 2023.

I am also Egyptian, which has fueled my research interest in Arabic! For more details, check my CV or send me an email. I am currently working on two blogs; From Linguist to Computational Linguist & How to Chair a Graduate Conference.
