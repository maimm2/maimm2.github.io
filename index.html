I am a Computational Linguistics PhD student at University of Illinois Urbana Champaign, where I work with my advisor, Professor Lane Schwartz. I am interested in working on problems that focus on language models related to Arabic morpho-syntax. My dissertation focuses on Classical Arabic corpora and datasets, Classical Arabic pre-processing tools, and training Neural Polysynthetic Language Modelling on various Arabic datasets.

I am also working with Professor Tania Ionin on an experiment exploring the effect of writing direction bias on mental representation of time in Arabic and English bilinguals.

I have been fortunate to work with well-established professors at UIUC. I did my MA thesis where I was advised by Randall Sadler. My MA thesis was conducted by collecting Tweets using Twitter API and analyzing them based on The Investment Model as a framework to explore how Egyptian Twitter users position themselves using English and Arabic on the platform. I graduated with a Bachelors in English Literature, Linguistics, and Education from Suez Canal University, Ismailia, Egypt in 2016 (Ranked 1st in class).
