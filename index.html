I am a third year Computational Linguistics PhD student at University of Illinois Urbana Champaign, where I am I advised by Dr. Schwartz. My current interests are computational modelling of Arabic dialects, and my dissertation focuses on modelling Arabic morpho-syntax.

I earned my MA in teaching English as a Second Language from UIUC in 2020. I also graduated with a Bachelors in English Literature, Linguistics, and Education from Suez Canal University, Ismailia, Egypt in 2016 (Ranked 1st in class). My research experience includes experimental research exploring mental representation of time among Arabic and English bilinguals advised by Dr. Ionin. I have also researched second language acquisition among Egyptian Twitter users advised by Dr. Sadler and Dr. Yan.

I am currently chairing the Annual Illinois Language and Linguistics Society Conference, taking place at UIUC on March 3rd, and March 4th, 2023.

I am also Egyptian, which has fueled my research interest in Arabic! For more details, check my CV or send me an email. Feel free to reach to chat about Arabic, either linguistically or computationally!
